Research Interests

Semiparametric Regression for Competing Risks

I established semiparametric transformation models for the subdistribution in competing risks analysis, substantially extending the widely used Fine-Gray model. My methods enable flexible regression modeling under weaker assumptions using a weighted nonparametric maximum likelihood estimation framework. The methodology was further extended to left-truncated and right-censored data, with applications to a large bone marrow transplant dataset and a large HIV vaccine efficacy trial.

Recurrent Events with Competing Terminal Events

General semiparametric regression models for recurrent event processes subject to competing terminal events, with application to data from a large COPD trial.

Personalized Risk Prediction after Stem Cell Transplantation

Collaborated in a large multicenter cohort study to assess risks associated with hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) as a treatment for high-risk hematologic malignancies in older subjects. The team established personalized prediction for non-relapse mortality after HSCT, resulting in the CHARM model.

Transfer Learning & Machine Learning

Current research interests include transfer learning approaches for clinical trials with sparse data, including applications in pediatric hematology and oncology.
